Team 13TRIS

13TRIS is the Project we 5 developed for the lecture of Software engineering 1&2 at the during the course of two semesters.

13TRIS is meant to be a dummy project for us to develop, while at the same time playing the roles of the project management team. It's a great way to get a glimpse of how companies manage the development of their software and all the thoughts that go into resource planning. The goal was not to programm however we wanted, in a chaotic and thoughtless way, but to structure and lay out a plan / future steps for the development process.

13TRIS is the world's (maybe not) first block placing game with a special twist !
13TRIS is the name of our spin-off from the classic game Tetris, but with some features and cool things added. How to play a solo game (an in-depth guide):
  1. Login with your own account here.
    (Note: if you chose to play without an account, visit the solo game page and continue this tutorial.
  2. Either press the enter key, or use the mouse cursor and aim somewhere near (chrome users need to aim lower than usual, I really don't know why) the 'START' and press the primary button.
  3. Use the arrow keys on your functional keyboard to move the falling blocks to your will.

    • Up is used for nothing, but I didn't feel like removing this key from the list, so it stays for no good reason.
    • Left is used to move the block one tile to the right. The other right !
    • Pressing down will move the current block one tile down, without having to wait on the slooow timer to do it automatically... Useless piece of silicon not even doing everything by itself.
    • Right is used to move the block one tile to the right as well ! If they moved left, it wouldn't be .. right. You only can win !!1! No need to thank me.
  4. While playing solo, you can always press esc to pause the game (and have some settings to play with, if you're bored with the game already).
  5. Repeat the above until the new block has no space to spawn (which is called spawn killing), and end the game with a total score of { % user.game.score % } !!
